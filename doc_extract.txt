2. Proposed System
2.1 Scope & Objective
The Proposed System, titled BHARATSTOCK: Retail Management System, is designed to be a comprehensive
digital ecosystem that redefines how small-scale retail and wholesale enterprises manage their day-to-day
operations. The core objective is to bridge the gap between traditional " book-Khata" (manual bookkeeping) and
modern Enterprise Resource Planning (ERP) systems.
1. Scope:
BharatStock is designed to be a comprehensive, 360-degree digital solution for Indian retail operations,
focusing specifically on the needs of small and medium-sized business owners who face daily challenges in
managing stock, computing taxes, and maintaining financial records. The scope of the system is defined by
five major operational pillars, each addressing a critical gap in the current landscape of retail management
in India:
Authentication & Multi-Tenancy:
Every business registered on BharatStock is isolated within a secure businessId
sandbox maintained within Google Cloud Firestore. This architecture ensures
that even when thousands of businesses simultaneously use the platform, each
business's sensitive financial data, stock records, and client information remain
completely private, protected, and inaccessible to other users. Multi-tenancy is
achievedatthedatabase-ruleslevelusing FirebaseSecurityRules.
Inventory & Stock Auditing:
BharatStock includes a real-time inventory management module that tracks
stock levels, purchase prices, selling prices, and profit margins for every product.
The system includes automated low-stock alert notifications to prevent stock-
out situations, which are a common cause of lost revenue for small retailers.
Product data can be imported in bulk or added individually with HSN codes for
taxcompliance.
Billing & Invoice Dispatch:
The application features a powerful digital billing engine capable of generating
high-quality PDF invoices with complete GST breakdowns (CGST, SGST, IGST).
These invoices are automatically pushed to a B2B Interaction Hub, where
supplier counterparts can view and approve incoming bills, enabling a
streamlineddigitalsupply chainworkflow.
Analytics & Financial Intelligence:
A dedicated analytics module tracks monthly profit/loss summaries, GST liability
calculations, Input Tax Credit (ITC) reconciliation, and best-selling product
identification — providing owners with actionable business intelligence without
requiringanyknowledge of accounting.

2. Objectives:
The primary objectives of BharatStock are carefully crafted to address the real-world pain points experienced
by Indian retail business owners on a daily basis:
a. Financial Digitization: To completely eliminate the losses associated with paper-based record-keeping,
including records damaged by fire, water, or physical theft. BharatStock stores all financial data securely
on Google's encrypted cloud infrastructure, ensuring permanent, accessible, and tamper-proof records.
b. Accuracy in Tax Compliance: To automate the highly complex and error-prone process of computing GST
splits between intra-state transactions (CGST + SGST) and inter-state transactions (IGST), ensuring that
the retailer never underpays or overpays GST amounts. The system auto-detects the appropriate tax
category based on the buyer's and seller's state codes.
c. Universal Accessibility: By supporting multiple regional languages including Gujarati and Hindi in addition
to English, BharatStock is designed to reach business owners in rural, suburban, and semi-urban areas
of India who are not comfortable with English-only interfaces.
d. GSTR Compliance Reporting: The system auto-generates GSTR-1 and GSTR-3B compatible reports,
dramatically reducing the time and cost associated with quarterly and monthly GST filing.
e. Improved Cash Flow Management: Through real-time ledger tracking, owners can at any moment see
exactly which customers owe them money (outstanding receivables) and which suppliers they need to
pay (outstanding payables), enabling proactive cash flow management.

3. System Analysis
System analysis is the systematic investigation and decomposition of an existing problem domain and the modeling
of a proposed solution. It is arguably the most critical phase of the entire software development lifecycle, as errors
or omissions made during analysis propagate forward into design and implementation, where they become
exponentially more expensive to correct.
For BharatStock, the system analysis phase involved direct observation of retail billing workflows, extensive
interviews with Kirana shop owners in Surat's commercial areas, competitive benchmarking against existing solutions
such as Vyapar, MyBillBook, and TallyPrime, and review of the Government of India's GST digitalization mandate
documents.
3.1 Existing System (Manual)
The term 'Khata' (meaning 'ledger' or 'account book') describes the traditional paper-based bookkeeping system
that has dominated Indian retail for centuries and continues to dominate the daily operations of millions of small
shop owners. Despite the significant advances in digital technology, a vast majority of Kirana stores, hardware
shops, and wholesale traders continue to rely exclusively on handwritten registers for their critical business
records. An analysis of this existing system reveals a comprehensive set of systemic failures that directly
motivated the development of BharatStock:
1. Physical Vulnerability of Records: Paper registers are inherently fragile. A single incident — fire, water
damage from monsoon flooding, theft, or simple accidental destruction — can permanently erase years of
irreplaceable financial records. There is no backup, no version history, and no recovery mechanism. Many
shop owners interviewed during the research phase recounted instances of significant financial loss directly
attributable to destroyed paper records.
2. Manual GST Calculation Errors: The Indian GST system requires the correct application of five different tax
slabs (0%, 5%, 12%, 18%, and 28%) across thousands of product categories, each with a specific HSN code.
When calculating manually, errors in applying the correct rate or in computing CGST vs. SGST splits are
extremely common. Such errors can result in GST penalties, notices from tax authorities, and loss of Input
Tax Credit eligibility.
3. Absence of Real-Time Inventory Visibility: With a paper system, a shopkeeper has no real-time mechanism
to know the precise current stock level of any product. Stock counts are performed periodically — often
weekly or monthly — meaning that stock-outs are frequently discovered only at the point of a customer
asking for an unavailable item. This leads to lost sales and customer dissatisfaction.
4. Inability to Search Historical Records: Retrieving a specific invoice from six months prior in a stack of physical
register books can take anywhere from thirty minutes to several hours. This makes dispute resolution with
customers, responses to tax authority queries, and credit verification of trade partners extremely time-
consuming and operationally disruptive.

3.2 Need for New System
The inadequacies of the existing Khata system, combined with several powerful external forces, create a
compelling and urgent need for a modern digital replacement:
1. Regulatory Mandate: The Government of India's GST digitalization initiative mandates electronic invoice
generation and submission for businesses above certain turnover thresholds. As these thresholds are
progressively lowered, an increasing proportion of small retailers will soon be legally required to maintain
digital invoicing systems.
2. Customer Expectations: Customers in urban and semi-urban India increasingly expect digital receipts, the
ability to track purchases, and the convenience of WhatsApp-based billing. Business owners who cannot
provide these experiences risk losing customers to more digitally capable competitors.
3. Financial Insights for Survival: In a highly competitive retail environment with thin margins, the ability to
quickly identify which products are profitable, which are underperforming, and where costs can be optimized
can mean the difference between business survival and closure. BharatStock puts this intelligence directly in
the owner's hands.
4. Post-Pandemic Digital Shift: The COVID-19 pandemic accelerated digital adoption across all segments of
Indian business. Many shop owners who previously resisted digital tools now recognize their value and are
actively seeking solutions that are simple, affordable, and tailored to Indian business practices.

4.1 Requirement Analysis & Data Gathering
1. Methodology
The requirement analysis for BharatStock employed a multi-method approach designed to capture a
comprehensive and representative picture of the real-world retail management challenges faced by the
target user base:
a. User Interviews: In-depth, semi-structured interviews were conducted with owners of Kirana stores and
textile traders operating in Surat districts. These interviews focused on understanding daily billing
workflows, common pain points with paper-based systems, attitude toward digital adoption, and feature
priorities.
b. Contextual Inquiry: Researchers spent time directly observing billing operations at retail counters,
watching how bills are manually calculated, how GST is applied, how customer queries are handled, and
how daily closing of accounts is performed. This observational approach revealed several implicit
requirements that the shop owners themselves had not articulated.
c. Regulatory Research: The GST Council's published guidelines, GSTR filing formats, and e-invoicing
mandate documentation were studied to ensure that BharatStock's billing engine and reporting module
would be compliant with both current and anticipated future regulatory requirements.
2. Data Sources & Validation
Requirement data was collected from a sample of 2 shop owners representing a diverse cross-section of
retail categories (grocery, hardware, textile, electronics), business sizes (annual turnover ranging around ₹3
lakh), and digital literacy levels (complete novice to basic smartphone user). Requirements were validated
through two rounds of prototype feedback sessions, during which early wireframe designs were presented
to a subset of the interviewed shop owners for critique and refinement.
3. System Constraints & Considerations
The following constraints were identified and factored into the system design during the planning phase:
a. Connectivity Dependency: Real-time synchronization of billing and inventory data requires internet
access. A minimum 4G mobile data connection is needed to save and sync transactions. Offline
queuing is provided for resilience.
b. Platform Priority: The initial release targets Android, which commands approximately 95% of the
Indian smartphone market. iOS support is planned as a secondary release phase.
c. Firebase Free-Tier Limits: For the pilot deployment, the application is designed to operate within
Firebase's Spark (free) tier limits, necessitating careful optimization of Firestore read/write
operations.

Firebase (Server-Side)
Firebase is Google's comprehensive Backend as a Service (BaaS) platform, providing a unified suite of cloud
infrastructure services designed to accelerate mobile and web application development. Firebase was originally
founded as an independent startup in 2011 and was acquired by Google in 2014, after which it was expanded
into the robust, enterprise-grade platform it is today.
For BharatStock, Firebase serves as the complete backend infrastructure, replacing the need for a traditional
server-side application stack. The specific Firebase services utilized are:
1. Cloud Firestore: A highly scalable, serverless, NoSQL document database that provides real-time data
synchronization across all connected clients. Firestore stores data in a hierarchical collection → document →
subcollection structure, making it naturally suited to BharatStock's business → products/bills/staff hierarchy.
Firestore's powerful Security Rules system enforces data access control at the database level, ensuring
complete multi-tenant data isolation.
2. Firebase Authentication: Manages the full lifecycle of user identity, including registration, login, session token
management, and secure password reset flows. For BharatStock, Firebase Auth handles the authentication
of both Owners and Staff, with custom claims used to store role information in the JWT token that each user
session carries.
3. Cloud Functions for Firebase: A serverless compute environment (running Node.js) that allows BharatStock
to execute trusted server-side logic in response to Firestore database events, HTTP requests, or scheduled
triggers. Key use cases include generating globally unique, sequential invoice IDs, sending automated push
notifications when stock levels fall below minimum thresholds, and computing aggregated monthly financial
summaries.
4. Firebase Cloud Storage: Object storage service used to persist business logo images and finalized PDF invoice
files, which are then served to users via secure, expiring download URLs.
5. Firebase Cloud Messaging (FCM): A cross-platform push notification delivery service used to deliver real-time
low-stock alerts, B2B interaction updates, and payment reminders to the app.

8. Limitations
While BharatStock represents a significant advancement over existing paper-based and basic digital retail
management tools, it is important to transparently document the current limitations of the system. These limitations
are acknowledged by the development team and are targeted for resolution in subsequent development phases:
Offline Dependency
Android Only
No Offline Bills
English Invoice Only
Firebase Cost
No Tally Integration
1. Internet Connectivity Dependency: As a cloud-first application built on Firebase's real-time infrastructure,
BharatStock requires a minimum 2G mobile data connection to perform write operations such as saving bills,
updating stock, and dispatching B2B interactions. While read operations benefit from Firestore's built-in offline
persistence cache — allowing users to browse existing products and view past invoices — new transactions
cannot be finalized and synced without network access. This is a meaningful limitation in regions of India where
mobile connectivity remains unreliable, particularly during monsoon seasons when network infrastructure may
be disrupted.
2. Android-First Platform Support: The current release version of BharatStock is optimized and tested for Android
devices. While Flutter's cross-platform architecture theoretically enables iOS compilation from the same
codebase, the iOS build requires additional configuration (Apple Developer account, code signing certificates,
App Store review compliance) and device-specific testing that extends beyond the scope of the current project
phase. iOS support is planned as an explicit deliverable in the next development cycle.
3. No Offline New-Bill Creation: While product browsing and historical bill viewing are available offline, creating
and saving new bills is currently blocked when the device has no internet connectivity. Implementing a robust
offline bill queue — where bills are created locally and atomically synced and reconciled when connectivity is
restored — requires careful handling of stock quantity race conditions and invoice ID sequencing, and is planned
as a future enhancement.

4. Single-Language Invoice: Currently, PDF invoices are generated in English only. The application UI supports Hindi
and Gujarati through Flutter's localization system, but the PDF generation library requires additional
configuration to render Devanagari and Gujarati Unicode characters correctly in the PDF output. Multi-language
invoice generation is a planned enhancement.
5. Firebase Pricing Threshold: While Firebase's free Spark tier is sufficient for early-stage deployment, businesses
with high transaction volumes (above approximately 20,000 Firestore reads/writes per day) will encounter
Firebase's paid Blaze plan thresholds. For medium-to-large retailers, operational costs on Firebase's pay-as-you-
go Blaze plan must be factored into the total cost of ownership.
6. No Built-In Accounting Integration: BharatStock currently does not provide direct integration with popular Indian
accounting software packages such as Tally Prime or QuickBooks. Financial data must be exported as CSV reports
and manually imported into these systems by the user, which introduces an additional step in the accounting
workflow for retailers who rely on separate accounting software.

9. Future Enhancement
The BharatStock development roadmap includes a rich set of planned enhancements that will significantly expand
the system's capabilities, reach, and intelligence. These enhancements are prioritized based on the feedback
collected during user testing sessions and the evolving regulatory environment:
AI

1. AI-Powered Inventory Intelligence: Integrating a machine learning-based demand forecasting engine that
analyses historical sales velocity, seasonal trends, and external market signals (such as upcoming festivals or
holidays) to predict which products are likely to face stock shortages in the coming 7–14 days. This predictive
capability will allow shop owners to proactively place procurement orders before stock-outs occur, eliminating
lost revenue events.
2. Integrated Digital Payments: Incorporating the Razorpay or PhonePe SDK to enable direct digital payment
collection through UPI, card, and net banking channels within the BharatStock bill confirmation flow. Upon
payment completion, the system will automatically mark the invoice as 'paid' and send a payment confirmation
receipt to the customer via WhatsApp — creating a fully paperless, end-to-end digital transaction experience.
3. Tally Prime Direct Export: Developing a certified XML export module that produces Tally-Prime-compatible
voucher files from BharatStock's sales and purchase data. This integration will allow retailers who maintain a
parallel Tally accounting system to automatically synchronize their BharatStock transaction data without manual
re-entry, eliminating a significant source of data entry duplication and error.
4. Multi-Language PDF Invoice Generation: Extending the PDF invoice generation engine to render invoices in Hindi
and Gujarati in addition to English, using proper Unicode font embedding and right-to-left text layout where
applicable. This will allow retailers to provide professionally formatted invoices in their customers' preferred
regional languages.
5. Offline-First Architecture with Conflict Resolution: Redesigning the bill creation and stock update workflows to
operate fully offline, with intelligent conflict resolution logic that handles concurrent stock modifications from
multiple staff members when connectivity is restored. This will make BharatStock viable for use in remote retail
environments where internet access is intermittent.
6. iOS App Release: Building, configuring, and submitting the BharatStock iOS application to the Apple App Store,
making the platform accessible to the segment of Indian business owners who use Apple devices.
